Inseung Kang

Hi! I'm Inseung Kang. I'm currently a research scientist at Exoskeleton and Prosthetic Intelligent Controls (EPIC) Lab at the Georgia Institute of Technology working with Dr. Aaron J. Young where I recently received my PhD degree. I mainly work on developing different control algorithms for robotic exoskeleton control. My research portfolio is fairly diverse ranging from designing versatile mechatronic hardware to developing a robust model (often integrating deep learning techniques). But mostly, my work revolves around better understanding human movement and designing wearable systems that allow improved mobility. My long-term research goal is to develop wearable interventions that are of good value to people with limited mobility.

Research

I'm interested in Robotics, Control, Machine Learning, Optimization, and Biomechanics. Much of my research explores how to optimize control strategies for maximizing human outcome measures when using wearable systems such as robotic exoskeletons. Mainly, I tackle these research questions by developing advanced inferences that can estimate and predict the user's intent during locomotion. Using these state variables, I can control exoskeletons with optimal assistance, enabling dynamic locomotion.
